# Household Image Sheet Rules

This file defines the rules for creating six-scene household asset sheets.

The asset style is a soft 3D cartoon illustration style for a playful family app.

---

## Standard Output

The default output is one single 3×2 image.

The image contains six landscape cells.

The default scene order is:

| Row        | Cell 1    | Cell 2    |
| ---------- | --------- | --------- |
| Top row    | Morning   | Midday    |
| Middle row | Afternoon | Evening   |
| Bottom row | Night     | Rainy Day |

The final image must not contain visible grid lines, dividers, borders, labels, or captions.

---

## Required Scenes

Every household image sheet contains exactly six scenes:

1. Morning
2. Midday
3. Afternoon
4. Evening
5. Night
6. Rainy Day

Do not omit scenes.

Do not add extra scenes unless the user specifically asks.

---

## Scene Definitions

### Morning

The household should appear in soft morning light.

Suitable examples:

- peach-pink sky
- low gentle sun
- soft warm light
- fresh early-day feeling
- calm and cheerful atmosphere

---

### Midday

The household should appear in clear daytime light.

Suitable examples:

- pale blue sky
- soft clouds
- high gentle sun
- bright readable lighting
- cheerful daytime atmosphere

---

### Afternoon

The household should appear in warm afternoon light.

Suitable examples:

- warmer blue sky
- gentle golden highlights
- slightly longer soft shadows
- relaxed cosy feeling
- welcoming atmosphere

---

### Evening

The household should appear in soft sunset light.

Suitable examples:

- apricot, coral, or lavender sky
- low warm sun
- soft cool shadows
- gentle window glow if appropriate
- calm settling-down feeling

---

### Night

The household should appear at night.

Suitable examples:

- deep blue or indigo sky
- crescent moon
- small stars
- warm glowing windows
- soft readable moonlight

The night scene must remain cosy, safe, and readable.

Avoid making it spooky or too dark.

---

### Rainy Day

The household should appear on a gentle rainy day.

Suitable examples:

- soft grey-blue sky
- light rain
- wet roof
- wet path or ground
- small puddles
- subtle reflections
- warm window glow if appropriate

The rainy scene must remain cosy and charming.

Avoid storms, lightning, flooding, danger, or gloomy drama.

---

## Visual Style

All images must use this style:

Soft 3D cartoon illustration, children’s-book app aesthetic, gentle cel shading with subtle ambient occlusion, rounded forms, soft edges, no hard black outlines, warm pastel palette, playful family-app feel, clean and readable at small size.

The final result should feel like a charming storybook illustration mixed with soft clay-like 3D animation.

---

## Style Must Include

- soft 3D cartoon look
- rounded forms
- soft edges
- gentle cel shading
- subtle ambient occlusion
- warm pastel colours
- cosy domestic feeling
- simplified household shapes
- clean readable silhouette
- playful app-friendly charm

---

## Style Must Avoid

Do not create:

- realistic render
- photorealistic image
- real estate photography
- architectural visualisation
- realistic architecture
- harsh 3D render
- glossy plastic toy render
- flat vector art
- anime
- manga
- comic-book art
- pixel art
- sketch art
- painterly concept art
- low-poly art
- dark cinematic lighting
- hard black outlines

---

## Canvas Rules

Every cell must be landscape.

Every cell must be the same size.

Each cell should be 1500px wide × 1000px high.

The full image should be 3000px wide × 3000px high.

The household must be centred in each cell.

The full household should be visible unless the user asks for a crop.

The household should fill the frame nicely without touching the edges.

---

## Background Rules

The background should show the household setting.

The setting can be anything the user requests.

Examples:

- town
- roadside
- countryside
- beach
- woodland
- city
- village
- mountain
- canal
- farm
- moon
- space colony
- fantasy world

The setting must remain the same in every cell.

The setting should be simple, soft, and uncluttered.

---

## Text Rules

The image must contain no text.

Do not include:

- labels
- captions
- scene names
- speech bubbles
- signs
- house numbers
- letters
- numbers
- logos
- UI elements

This applies to every cell.

---

## Grid Rules

The full image may be arranged as a 3×2 household sheet.

However, the grid must not be visibly drawn.

There must be:

- no borders
- no dividers
- no frames
- no cell outlines
- no labels
- no captions

The six landscape cells should simply sit together cleanly.

---

## Household Consistency Rules

The household must remain identical in all six cells.

Do not change:

- building type
- building shape
- proportions
- colours
- roof
- walls
- windows
- door
- chimney
- balcony
- porch
- steps
- fence
- path
- garden layout
- plants
- trees
- setting
- scale
- camera angle
- lighting style
- render style

Only the time of day, weather, sky, atmosphere, shadows, reflections, and window glow should change.

The sheet should look like one household shown in six different conditions.

It must not look like six different versions of a similar household.

---

## Camera Rules

Use the same camera angle across all six cells.

Preferred angle:

- front-facing
- or slight three-quarter view

Avoid:

- dramatic perspective
- top-down view
- side-only view
- extreme close-up
- inconsistent zoom
- cinematic angles

---

## Lighting Rules

Lighting should change naturally for each scene.

Lighting should always be:

- soft
- warm
- readable
- gentle
- app-friendly

Avoid:

- harsh shadows
- moody lighting
- dramatic lighting
- horror lighting
- over-dark night scenes
- inconsistent render style

---

## Environmental Rules

Environmental details should react to the time of day and weather.

Suitable examples:

- windows glow at night
- porch lights glow in evening or rain
- paths become wet in rain
- puddles appear in rain
- plants look darker and glossier in rain
- sky colour changes by time of day
- shadows change by time of day
- moon and stars appear at night

Do not add major new objects in only one cell.

Do not remove fixed setting details between cells.

## Setting Clarification Rules

Before producing a final household prompt, make sure you know:

1. Household design
2. Setting

If the household design is missing, ask:

“What should the household look like?”

If the setting is missing, ask:

“Where should it be set?”

If both are clear, generate the final prompt without asking unnecessary questions.

If the user asks you to choose, infer a suitable setting.

Examples:

- cottage, barn, farmhouse, cabin → countryside
- terrace, townhouse, apartment block → town
- detached, semi-detached, bungalow → suburban street
- beach house → beach
- houseboat or canal boat → canal or riverside
- chalet → mountain
- igloo → snowy landscape
- moon base → moon

---

## Enrichment Rules

If the user gives a simple household description, enrich it gently while preserving the core idea.

Examples:

- “cottage” can become “a small rounded storybook cottage with a soft slate roof, chunky chimney, warm windows, a little porch, and climbing flowers”
- “apartment block” can become “a friendly low-rise apartment building with rounded balconies, warm windows, potted plants, and a welcoming entrance”
- “houseboat” can become “a cosy rounded houseboat with small windows, a curved roof, soft railings, potted plants, and gentle nautical details”
- “moon base” can become “a cosy little moon-base home with rounded dome modules, warm glowing windows, a small airlock door, and soft antenna details”

Do not overcomplicate the household.

## Quality Checklist

Before finalising, check:

- Does the image contain exactly six scenes?
- Is the layout 3×2?
- Are the scenes in the correct order?
- Is each cell landscape?
- Is each cell 1500px wide × 1000px high?
- Is the full image 3000px wide × 3000px high?
- Is there no text?
- Are there no labels?
- Are there no visible cell borders?
- Are there no dividers?
- Is the same household shown in every cell?
- Is the same setting shown in every cell?
- Is the camera angle consistent?
- Is the scale consistent?
- Is the composition consistent?
- Does only the time, weather, light, sky, atmosphere, reflections, or glow change?
- Do fixed details remain in the same place?
- Is Rainy Day gentle rather than stormy?
- Is Night cosy and readable?
- Is the style soft 3D cartoon?
- Is the asset readable at small size?

---

## Most Important Rule

The household must look identical across all six scenes.

Consistency matters more than novelty.
